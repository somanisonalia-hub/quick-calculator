## **MANDATORY STRUCTURE** (Never Deviate)

### **Required Sections** (All Must Be Present)

1. **Introduction** (80-120 words)
   - Explain what the calculator does
   - Explain who it's designed for
   - Include primary keyword naturally ONCE
   - Mention key benefits briefly

2. **Benefits** (60-90 words)
   - 4-6 bullet points of key advantages
   - Focus on real user benefits
   - Explain why each benefit matters
   - No marketing fluff

3. **Steps** (50-70 words)
   - Clear numbered steps (3-5 steps)
   - Beginner-friendly language
   - Include action verbs
   - Natural long-tail keyword placement

4. **Inputs Explained** (70-100 words)
   - Explain each input field clearly
   - Explain WHY each input matters
   - Use simple analogies if helpful
   - Technical terms explained

5. **Formula Explanation** (80-120 words)
   - Break down the calculation conceptually
   - Explain the mathematical approach
   - Use real-world analogies
   - Avoid complex math symbols (explain in words)

6. **Examples** (100-150 words)
   - 2 realistic examples with actual numbers
   - Include 1 simple and 1 advanced scenario
   - Show real-world context
   - Natural long-tail keyword integration

7. **Results Explanation** (60-90 words)
   - Explain each output clearly
   - What users should look for
   - How to interpret results safely
   - Actionable insights

8. **Who It's For** (50-70 words)
   - Specific user personas
   - Practical use cases
   - Target audience segments

9. **Disclaimer** (40-60 words)
   - Accuracy limitations
   - Professional advice recommendation
   - Educational purpose statement
   - Legal compliance

10. **Related Tools** (30-50 words)
    - 2-3 related calculators by slug
    - Brief explanation of each
    - Natural linking suggestions

11. **Frequently Asked Questions** (Strongly Recommended)
    - 3-5 user-focused questions addressing real pain points
    - Practical, actionable answers with specific examples and numbers
    - Include long-tail keyword variations naturally
    - Focus on "how-to" and "what-if" scenarios
    - Questions should be based on actual user search intent and common concerns

**FAQ Examples for Mortgage Calculator:**
- "How do I calculate my monthly mortgage payment including taxes and insurance?"
- "What's the difference between a 15-year and 30-year mortgage?"
- "How much can I save by making extra payments?"
- "Can I use this mortgage calculator to plan refinancing?"
- "How does down payment affect my monthly payment?"

**FAQ Answer Guidelines:**
- Provide specific numbers and examples in answers
- Include step-by-step explanations where helpful
- Address common misconceptions
- Link to related concepts within the calculator
- Use natural language that matches user search queries

## **SEO REQUIREMENTS** (Critical)

### **Keyword Integration**
- **Primary Keyword**: Use in seoTitle, metaDescription, and Introduction (exactly once)
- **Long-tail Keywords**: Sprinkle naturally in Introduction, Steps, and Examples (2-3 total)
- **Natural Placement**: Never force keywords; they must fit conversationally

### **Content Volume**
- **Minimum 400 words** per page
- **Each section**: 50-80 words minimum
- **Examples section**: 100+ words (most detailed)

### **Meta Information**
- **seoTitle**: Include primary keyword + "Calculator" + compelling benefit
- **metaDescription**: 150-160 characters with primary keyword + key benefit

## **CONTENT QUALITY STANDARDS** (Non-Negotiable)

### **Writing Style**
- **Clear & Concise**: Short sentences, active voice
- **User-Focused**: Explain "why it matters" in every section
- **Educational**: Teach users something valuable
- **Authoritative**: Confident but not arrogant
- **Conversational**: Like explaining to a friend

### **Formatting Rules**
- **Bullet Points**: For benefits, inputs, results
- **Numbered Lists**: For steps and examples
- **Short Paragraphs**: Maximum 3-4 sentences each
- **Action-Oriented**: Use verbs like "calculate," "determine," "discover"

### **Avoid These** ‚ùå
- Marketing buzzwords ("revolutionary," "amazing," "best")
- Technical jargon without explanation
- Passive voice
- Complex sentences
- Repetitive phrasing
- Keyword stuffing
